How Debt to Asset Ratio Works

Formula

Debt to Asset Ratio = Total Debt ÷ Total Assets

This ratio shows what proportion of a company's assets are financed through debt.

What It Measures

  • Financial leverage and risk
  • Debt management efficiency
  • Capital structure analysis
  • Creditworthiness assessment

Key Insights

  • Lower ratios indicate lower financial risk
  • Higher ratios may limit borrowing capacity
  • Industry context is crucial for interpretation
  • Trend analysis is more valuable than single point

Example Calculation

Company ABC:

Total Debt: $500,000

Total Assets: $1,000,000

Debt to Asset Ratio: $500,000 ÷ $1,000,000 = 0.50 or 50%

This means 50% of the company's assets are financed through debt.
