Dividend Yield Ranges

Low Yield (0-2%)

Growth companies or overpriced stocks. May indicate capital appreciation focus.

Moderate Yield (2-4%)

Balanced companies with steady dividend payments and growth potential.

High Yield (4%+)

Income-focused stocks, REITs, or potentially distressed companies.

Formula

Dividend Yield = (Annual Dividend Per Share ÷ Stock Price) × 100

Annual Income = Dividend Per Share × Number of Shares

Annual Dividend Per Share: Total dividends paid per share over one year

Stock Price: Current market price per share

Result: Percentage return from dividends relative to stock price

Investment Tips

  • Higher yields aren't always better - investigate sustainability
  • Consider dividend growth history and payout ratios
  • Reinvesting dividends can compound returns over time
  • Compare yields within the same industry sector
  • Factor in taxes on dividend income in your calculations
