Understanding Carried Interest

Key Concepts

  • Carried Interest: GP's share of fund profits, typically 20%
  • Hurdle Rate: Minimum return LPs must receive before carry
  • Catch-up: Allows GP to receive carry on early distributions
  • Waterfall: Order of distributions between LPs and GP

Waterfall Types

  • American: Carry calculated on each deal
  • European: Carry calculated on total fund performance
  • Management Fee: Annual fee, typically 2% of committed capital
  • Clawback: GP must return excess carry if fund underperforms

Example Waterfall Structure

1. Return of LP capital contributions

2. Payment of preferred return (hurdle) to LPs

3. Catch-up to GP until 20% of distributions received

4. 80/20 split between LPs and GP on remaining distributions
