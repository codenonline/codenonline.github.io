Understanding Gift of Equity

What is Gift of Equity?

A gift of equity occurs when a seller sells their property to a family member or close friend for less than the fair market value. The difference between the market value and the sale price represents the gift of equity.

How It Works

  • Property is appraised to determine fair market value
  • Seller agrees to sell for less than market value
  • The difference becomes the gift of equity
  • Buyer can often use this as part of their down payment

Benefits

  • Helps buyers who may not have enough cash for down payment
  • Can eliminate need for PMI if gift creates 20%+ equity
  • Keeps property within family
  • May provide tax benefits for sellers in some situations

Requirements

  • Professional appraisal required to establish market value
  • Gift letter required by lender
  • Usually limited to family members or close relationships
  • Property must be buyer's primary residence (for most loan programs)

Calculation Formula

Gift of Equity = Market Value - Sale Price
Seller Net Proceeds = Sale Price - Outstanding Mortgage - Closing Costs
Buyer's Effective Down Payment = Cash Down Payment + Gift of Equity
