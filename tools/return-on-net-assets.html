Understanding RONA

What is RONA?

Return on Net Assets (RONA) measures how efficiently a company uses its net assets to generate profits. It shows how much profit is generated for each dollar of net assets.

Formula

RONA = Net Income ÷ Net Assets × 100

Where Net Assets = Total Assets - Current Liabilities - Non-Interest Bearing Liabilities

Good RONA Values

  • • Above 15%: Excellent
  • • 10-15%: Good
  • • 5-10%: Average
  • • Below 5%: Poor

Key Benefits

  • • Measures asset efficiency
  • • Compares companies across industries
  • • Identifies operational improvements
  • • Evaluates management performance
  • • Guides investment decisions

Limitations

  • • Based on book values, not market values
  • • Can be manipulated by asset sales
  • • Doesn't consider risk factors
  • • May not reflect future performance
  • • Varies by industry and business model

Pro Tips

  • • Compare RONA with industry averages for better context
  • • Track RONA trends over multiple periods
  • • Use alongside other profitability ratios like ROA and ROE
  • • Consider the company's growth stage when evaluating RONA
  • • Look for consistency in RONA performance over time
