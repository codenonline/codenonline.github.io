Understanding Leverage Ratios

Leverage ratios measure the extent to which a company uses debt financing relative to equity financing. These ratios help investors and analysts assess a company's financial risk and ability to meet its debt obligations.

Key Points:

  • Higher leverage ratios indicate higher financial risk
  • Optimal leverage varies by industry and economic conditions
  • Companies with stable cash flows can typically handle higher leverage
  • Too much leverage can lead to financial distress during downturns
  • Consider multiple leverage ratios for comprehensive analysis
