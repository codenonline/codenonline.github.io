Formula Used

CAGR = (Target Value / Initial Value)^(1/Years) - 1

Where:

• Target Value = Desired final amount

• Initial Value = Starting investment

• Years = Investment time period

• Result is expressed as a percentage

Key Insights

  • • Higher growth rates become exponentially harder to achieve
  • • Longer time periods reduce required annual growth rates
  • • Consider risk tolerance when evaluating feasibility
  • • Market averages: S&P 500 ~10%, Bonds ~3-5%
  • • Growth rates above 15% annually are typically high-risk
  • • Use this to set realistic investment goals and timelines
