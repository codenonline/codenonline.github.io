Understanding Wind Turbine Investments

Key Formulas

Annual Energy Production:
Capacity × Number of Turbines × 8760 hours × Capacity Factor
LCOE:
(Initial Investment + PV of O&M Costs) ÷ PV of Energy Production
NPV:
Sum of (Annual Cash Flow ÷ (1 + Discount Rate)^Year) - Initial Investment

Investment Factors

  • Wind Resource: Higher capacity factors mean better wind conditions and more revenue
  • Location: Consider transmission costs, grid access, and local electricity prices
  • Technology: Larger, more efficient turbines often have better economics
  • Incentives: Tax credits, renewable energy certificates, and subsidies can improve returns
  • Power Purchase Agreements: Long-term contracts can provide stable revenue
