How It Works

Direct Method

Formula:

OCF = Cash Receipts - Cash Payments

Lists actual cash inflows and outflows from operating activities.

Advantages:

  • Provides detailed cash flow information
  • Shows actual cash receipts and payments
  • More intuitive and easier to understand

Indirect Method

Formula:

OCF = Net Income + Depreciation - ΔReceivables - ΔInventory + ΔPayables + Other

Adjusts net income for non-cash items and working capital changes.

Advantages:

  • More commonly used in practice
  • Links net income to cash flow
  • Easier to prepare from financial statements

Key Points:

  • • Operating cash flow measures cash generated from core business operations
  • • Positive OCF indicates the company generates sufficient cash to maintain operations
  • • OCF is crucial for assessing financial health and sustainability
  • • Both methods should yield the same result when calculated correctly
