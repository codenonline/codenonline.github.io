Formula

EAR = (1 + r/n)^n - 1
Where:
• EAR = Effective Annual Rate
• r = Nominal interest rate (decimal)
• n = Number of compounding periods per year
Continuous Compounding:
EAR = e^r - 1

Key Concepts

Effective Annual Rate (EAR):

The actual annual rate of return, accounting for compounding frequency. Also known as Annual Percentage Yield (APY).

Nominal Rate:

The stated annual interest rate without considering compounding effects.

Compounding Frequency:

How often interest is calculated and added to the principal. More frequent compounding results in higher effective rates.

Why It Matters:

EAR allows accurate comparison between investments with different compounding frequencies.
