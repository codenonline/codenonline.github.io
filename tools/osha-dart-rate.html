DART Rate Formula & Information

Formula

Number of DART Cases × 200,000
Total Hours Worked

The constant 200,000 represents the number of hours 100 full-time employees would work in a year (50 weeks × 40 hours × 100 employees).

DART Cases Include

  • Days Away from Work cases
  • Restricted Work Activity cases
  • Job Transfer cases
  • Medical treatment only cases

Tips for Reducing DART Rate

Prevention Strategies

  • Implement comprehensive safety training programs
  • Conduct regular workplace hazard assessments
  • Provide proper personal protective equipment
  • Establish clear safety protocols and procedures

Return-to-Work Programs

  • Develop modified duty assignments
  • Partner with healthcare providers
  • Maintain regular communication with injured workers
  • Track and analyze injury trends
