Understanding Contribution Margin

Key Concepts

Contribution Margin: The amount remaining from sales revenue after variable costs are subtracted. This contributes to covering fixed costs and generating profit.
Variable Costs: Costs that change directly with the level of production or sales volume (materials, direct labor, sales commissions).
Contribution Margin Ratio: The percentage of each sales dollar available to cover fixed costs and profit.

Formulas Used

Contribution Margin = Revenue - Variable Costs
Contribution Margin Ratio = (Contribution Margin ÷ Revenue) × 100
Contribution Margin per Unit = Selling Price - Variable Cost per Unit
Variable Cost Ratio = (Variable Costs ÷ Revenue) × 100

Applications

  • Pricing decisions and product profitability analysis
  • Break-even analysis and sales target setting
  • Product mix optimization
  • Cost-volume-profit analysis
  • Make-or-buy decisions
  • Performance evaluation and budgeting
