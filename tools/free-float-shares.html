Understanding Free Float Shares

What is Free Float?

Free float represents the portion of a company's shares that are available for public trading. These shares are not held by insiders, large institutional investors, or other restricted parties.

Key Components

  • Insider Holdings: Shares held by company executives, directors, and employees
  • Institutional Holdings: Large stakes (>5%) held by institutions
  • Treasury Shares: Shares repurchased by the company
  • Strategic Holdings: Long-term strategic investments

Why Free Float Matters

  • Liquidity: Higher free float means easier buying and selling
  • Volatility: Lower free float can lead to higher price volatility
  • Index Inclusion: Many indices use free-float weighting
  • Takeover Risk: Low free float may indicate takeover protection

Formula

Free Float = Total Outstanding Shares - Restricted Holdings

Analysis Tips

High Free Float (>75%)

Generally indicates good liquidity and institutional interest

Medium Free Float (25-75%)

Moderate liquidity, analyze holder composition carefully

Low Free Float (<25%)

May have liquidity issues and higher volatility
