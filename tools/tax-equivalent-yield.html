Understanding Tax-Equivalent Yield

Formula

TEY = Municipal Bond Yield / (1 − Marginal Tax Rate)

Key Concepts

Tax-Exempt Income

Municipal bond interest is typically exempt from federal tax and, for in-state bonds, often from state tax too.

Marginal Tax Rate

The tax rate applied to your last dollar of income. Higher brackets make munis increasingly attractive.

Break-Even Yield

If a taxable bond yields more than the TEY, choose the taxable bond. If less, the muni bond wins after tax.

When Municipal Bonds Are Most Attractive

  • High-income investors in the top federal tax brackets (32%, 35%, 37%)
  • Residents of high-tax states such as California, New York, New Jersey
  • Investors subject to the Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT)
  • Retirees seeking stable, tax-efficient income
  • When taxable bond spreads over Treasuries are compressed

Limitations

  • Some municipal bonds are subject to AMT — verify before investing
  • Capital gains from muni bond sales are still taxable
  • Tax laws change — recalculate after any rate changes
  • Does not account for credit risk differences between muni and taxable bonds
  • Social Security recipients may face additional taxation effects
