Perpetuity Formulas

Ordinary Perpetuity

PV = PMT ÷ r
  • • PV = Present Value
  • • PMT = Annual Payment
  • • r = Discount Rate (as decimal)

Growing Perpetuity

PV = PMT ÷ (r - g)
  • • PV = Present Value
  • • PMT = First Year Payment
  • • r = Discount Rate (as decimal)
  • • g = Growth Rate (as decimal)

Note: Growth rate must be less than discount rate

Key Concepts

What is a Perpetuity?

  • • Stream of equal payments forever
  • • Common in preferred stocks
  • • Used in real estate valuations
  • • Foundation for many financial models

Applications

  • • Valuing preferred shares
  • • Real estate income properties
  • • Pension valuations
  • • Terminal value calculations

Important Considerations

  • • Assumes payments continue forever
  • • Discount rate must be positive
  • • Growth rate must be less than discount rate
  • • Theoretical concept (infinite time)
