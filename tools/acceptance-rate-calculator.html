Insights & Interpretation

High Acceptance Rate

This indicates a relatively high success rate for applications. This could suggest either high-quality applicants or less stringent selection criteria.

Moderate Acceptance Rate

This represents a balanced selection process with moderate competition among applicants.

Low Acceptance Rate

This indicates highly competitive selection with stringent criteria. This is common in prestigious institutions or limited-capacity programs.

Understanding Acceptance Rates

Formula

Acceptance Rate = (Accepted Applications / Total Applications) × 100

Rejection Rate = (Rejected Applications / Total Applications) × 100

Common Use Cases

  • • University admissions
  • • Job applications
  • • Scholarship programs
  • • Grant applications
  • • Loan applications
  • • Contest submissions

Tips for Analysis

  • • Context matters: Compare rates within similar programs or industries
  • • Track trends: Monitor acceptance rates over time to identify patterns
  • • Consider quality: Lower rates don't always mean better programs
  • • Account for self-selection: Some pools may be pre-qualified

Typical Ranges

0-10%: Highly selective (Top universities, prestigious programs)

10-30%: Selective (Competitive programs)

30-70%: Moderately selective

70%+: Open/Less selective
