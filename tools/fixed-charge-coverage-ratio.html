Formula & Explanation

FCCR Formula:

FCCR = (EBIT + Lease Expenses) / (Interest + Lease Expenses + Principal Repayments/(1-Tax Rate) + Other Fixed Charges)

Components:

  • EBIT: Earnings Before Interest and Taxes
  • Interest: Interest expenses on debt
  • Lease Expenses: Operating and finance lease payments
  • Principal: Debt principal repayments
  • Tax Rate: Corporate tax rate

Interpretation Guide:

  • > 2.5: Excellent coverage
  • 2.0 - 2.5: Good coverage
  • 1.5 - 2.0: Adequate coverage
  • 1.2 - 1.5: Marginal coverage
  • < 1.2: Poor coverage

Understanding FCCR

What is FCCR?

The Fixed Charge Coverage Ratio measures a company's ability to cover all fixed charges, including interest, lease payments, and principal repayments, from its operating earnings.

Why is it Important?

  • • Assesses financial stability and solvency
  • • Used by lenders for credit decisions
  • • Indicates ability to service debt obligations
  • • Helps evaluate financial risk

Key Features:

  • • Includes all fixed financial charges
  • • Accounts for tax effects on principal payments
  • • More comprehensive than interest coverage ratio
  • • Critical for leveraged companies

Usage Tips:

  • • Compare with industry benchmarks
  • • Analyze trends over multiple periods
  • • Consider alongside other coverage ratios
  • • Review loan covenant requirements
