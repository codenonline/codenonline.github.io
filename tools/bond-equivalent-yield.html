Formula & Information

Discount Bond BEY Formula

BEY = [(Face Value - Purchase Price) / Purchase Price] × (365 / Days to Maturity)

Periodic Payment Bond BEY

BEY = 2 × [(1 + r/n)^n - 1]
where r = annual rate, n = compounding frequency

Key Concepts

  • Bond Equivalent Yield: Standardized yield measure for comparing bonds
  • Discount Bond: Sold below face value, no periodic payments
  • BEY Purpose: Enables comparison of bonds with different payment structures
  • Semi-annual Basis: BEY assumes semi-annual compounding

Usage Tips

  • • Use for comparing T-bills with coupon bonds
  • • Higher BEY indicates better yield for comparison
  • • Consider tax implications in actual decisions
  • • BEY ignores reinvestment risk

Understanding Bond Equivalent Yield

What is BEY?

Bond Equivalent Yield standardizes yields across different bond types by converting them to a semi-annual compounding basis, making comparison easier.

When to Use BEY

  • • Comparing Treasury bills with bonds
  • • Evaluating different investment options
  • • Portfolio yield analysis

BEY Limitations

  • • Assumes reinvestment at same rate
  • • Ignores credit risk differences
  • • Tax considerations not included
  • • Liquidity differences ignored

Alternative Measures

  • • Yield to Maturity (YTM)
  • • Effective Annual Yield
  • • Tax-Equivalent Yield
