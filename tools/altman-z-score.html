Interpretation

Bankruptcy Probability:

Altman Z-Score Formula

Z = 1.2A + 1.4B + 3.3C + 0.6D + 1.0E
A: Working Capital / Total Assets
B: Retained Earnings / Total Assets
C: Earnings Before Interest and Taxes / Total Assets
D: Market Value of Equity / Total Liabilities
E: Sales / Total Assets

Risk Zones

Safe Zone (Z > 2.99)
Low bankruptcy risk. Company is financially healthy.
Gray Zone (1.8 < Z < 2.99)
Moderate risk. Requires careful monitoring.
Distress Zone (Z < 1.8)
High bankruptcy risk. Immediate attention required.
Note: The Altman Z-Score was originally developed for manufacturing companies. Modified versions exist for service companies and non-public companies.

Usage Tips

Data Requirements

  • • Use most recent annual financial statements
  • • Ensure data consistency across all inputs
  • • Working Capital = Current Assets - Current Liabilities
  • • EBIT excludes interest and tax expenses

Limitations

  • • Originally designed for manufacturing firms
  • • May not apply well to service companies
  • • Should be used with other financial metrics
  • • Market conditions can affect accuracy
