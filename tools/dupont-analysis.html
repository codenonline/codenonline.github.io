Understanding DuPont Analysis

What is DuPont Analysis?

DuPont analysis breaks down Return on Equity (ROE) into three fundamental components to help identify what drives a company's profitability and financial performance.

1. Profit Margin (Profitability)

Measures how much profit the company generates from each dollar of sales

2. Asset Turnover (Efficiency)

Measures how efficiently the company uses its assets to generate sales

3. Equity Multiplier (Leverage)

Measures the company's financial leverage and use of debt

Interpretation Guidelines

High Profit Margin

Strong pricing power, efficient operations, or premium products

High Asset Turnover

Efficient asset utilization, fast inventory cycles

High Equity Multiplier

Higher leverage, potentially higher returns but also higher risk

ROE Benchmarks

15%+ is generally considered strong, but varies by industry
